## 2. Executive Summary

- Church leadership treats church structure as a core foundation module. The project is therefore moving Church Structure from a transitional dual-model state to the canonical core model, deliberately and in small slices.
- `ChurchStructureUnit` gradually becomes the canonical church structure tree. `ChurchStructureMembership` gradually becomes the canonical user belonging source.
- Legacy `MinistryContext`, `District`, and `SmallGroup` were the compatibility layer during migration and have now completed the guarded final table-retirement path for local/dev and the GoDaddy production target reported deployed on 2026-06-24. `Profile.small_group` was removed in PROFILE-SG-FIELD-RETIRE.1A (migration `accounts/0012`), and `accounts.0015_remove_legacy_structure_tables` removes the legacy object tables on targets that pass the zero-row guard.
- LEGACY-STRUCTURE-TABLE-RETIRE.1B locally verified `accounts.0015` as applied on the `E:\bible-reading\bible_reading_v2` dev DB on 2026-06-24. PROD-LEGACY-STRUCTURE-DEPLOY-CLOSE.1A records the human-reported successful GoDaddy production guarded deployment the same date. After `accounts.0015`, `SmallGroup`, `District`, and `MinistryContext` are removed from ORM/runtime and from the target schema. Other future target DBs still require the normal guarded deployment migration step and target-DB guard review before claiming the same state there.
- This has remained **gradual retirement, not a big-bang drop**. Legacy table/field removals and runtime source switches happened only through separately approved, tested slices with per-consumer rollback paths.
- The hardest remaining work is no longer table deletion for the already-closed local/dev and GoDaddy production targets. The next phase is product polish, structure setup UX, and ordinary release smoke. Switched so far: ServiceEvent structure-audience matching consults the active primary `ChurchStructureMembership` (CS-CORE.2B-A) and zero-row events fail closed after SE-RETIRE.1B; Bible Study v2 `BibleStudyMeeting` ordinary-member visibility consults active primary `ChurchStructureMembership` through `BibleStudyMeetingAudienceScope` rows and zero-row meetings fail closed after BS-STRUCT.2A; V1 `BibleStudySession` app runtime/admin are retired and BS-V1-SCHEMA-RETIRE.1A removes the V1 models/tables behind a migration guard, without V1-to-membership or V1-to-V2 migration; ordinary group-prayer access consults `PrayerRequest.structure_unit_at_post` after PRAYER-STRUCT.1A; the group-progress visible roster (`member_rows`) consults the membership-core candidate (CS-CORE.4F.1); the group-progress no-`?group=` default selected group consults a **permission-fenced** membership-core candidate (CS-CORE.4F.2); ordinary-member group **reflection read** visibility consults `structure_unit_at_post` (CS-CORE.4G.2); the reflection/comment **write path / form option-gating** consults active primary membership (CS-CORE.4G.3); and the group-progress **permission / accessible group list** consults structure-aware role scopes plus the membership-core own group (CS-CORE.2D-B). The ChurchRoleAssignment role/capability model itself remains, but scoped role runtime now requires explicit `structure_unit` after ROLE-RETIRE.1B, and the legacy `district` / `small_group` role fields were removed in ROLE-FIELD-RETIRE.1A (migration `accounts/0011`). These are separate, separately-approved slices. TeamAssignment / My Serving are **not** migrated to `ChurchStructureMembership` and remain a separate concept: serving is driven by the ministry/team assignment models and ServiceEvent context, not inferred from `Profile.small_group` or from membership (concept separation, Section 5).

## 4. Target Architecture

- **Canonical structure tree:** `ChurchStructureUnit` (single active whole-church root; typed, ordered, cycle-validated hierarchy).
- **Canonical belonging:** `ChurchStructureMembership`. Active, dated, approved membership rows (one active primary per user) are the runtime belonging answer for migrated ordinary-member paths.
- **Compatibility layer during migration:** `MinistryContext`, `District`, `SmallGroup`, and their `church_structure_unit` mapping FKs were the migration compatibility layer. For local/dev and for the GoDaddy production target reported deployed on 2026-06-24, the guarded object-row purge, LEGACY-STRUCTURE-SURFACE-RETIRE.1A, and `accounts.0015_remove_legacy_structure_tables` retire those active models/tables and mapping FKs. They must not be used as ordinary-member visibility authority. Other future target DBs must still run the guarded deployment flow before claiming the same schema state.
- **Audience selection stays per-module:** app-specific join models (`ServiceEventAudienceScope`, `BibleStudySeriesAudienceScope`, and future modules' own join models) selecting `ChurchStructureUnit` rows. No shared generic audience table, and no new legacy-only multi-select scope fields.
- **Final runtime matching target:** ordinary-user audience matching eventually compares the user's **active structure memberships** (plus unit ancestors/descendants as the rule requires) **directly against selected structure units, with no legacy small-group hop**. The unit-to-`SmallGroup` resolver then remains only where legacy rows are still the work target (e.g. meeting generation) until those consumers are migrated or retired.
- **Permissions and serving stay separate:** `ChurchRoleAssignment` + capabilities, and `TeamAssignment` / My Serving, are not migrated by CS-CORE. A structure-aware role scope is an optional, separate decision (CS-CORE.2D).

## 5. Concept Separation Rules (binding)

These six concerns must remain separate concepts, separate surfaces, and separate milestones. No single UI and no single milestone may mix them:

1. **Structure setup** — creating, renaming, moving, deactivating `ChurchStructureUnit` rows. Write surface today: Django Admin (plus the narrow CS-SETUP.1B rename slice). Future primary surface: CS-CORE.2A staff setup UI.
2. **Membership roster / user belonging** — who belongs to which unit; requests, approvals, transfers, end dates. Surfaces: signup/Profile request capture, staff approval queue, Django Admin; future roster UI is CS-CORE.2C, never embedded in structure setup.
3. **Audience selection** — per-event / per-schedule unit picks through module-specific join models and the shared picker. Belongs to each module's create/edit flow, never to structure setup.
4. **Legacy mapping diagnostics** — `/staff/structure/mappings/` review/edit. Migration diagnostics and advanced maintenance only. It is not, and must not become, the main staff setup UI; it is retired together with the mapping bridge.
5. **Permissions / role scopes** — `ChurchRoleAssignment` and the capability map. Membership never implies capability; structure setup never edits roles. (CS-CORE.2D-B migrated the narrow ordinary **own-group** group-progress view from `Profile.small_group` to the active primary membership; that is a belonging→own-group rule, not a role/capability grant, and broad progress scope still comes from `ChurchRoleAssignment` role scopes.)
6. **Serving assignments** — `TeamAssignment` / `TeamAssignmentMember` / My Serving. Independent of audience and belonging; membership never infers serving.

The existing binding rule that Audience Scope, Host / Language Label, Required Ministry Teams, Rotation Anchor Team, and TeamAssignment are distinct concepts remains in force unchanged.

1. Do not drop or truncate legacy tables outside their approved guarded migration. LEGACY-STRUCTURE-TABLE-RETIRE.1A removes `MinistryContext`, `District`, and `SmallGroup` only after the migration guard confirms zero legacy rows on the target DB.
2. `Profile.small_group` was **removed** in PROFILE-SG-FIELD-RETIRE.1A (migration `accounts/0012`) — see the current-state block in Section 1. Do not reintroduce the field or any normal app-level write to a legacy profile group field, and do not treat any such field as a membership source. `ChurchStructureMembership` (active primary) is the canonical belonging source for migrated runtime paths. After LEGACY-STRUCTURE-TABLE-RETIRE.1A applies, the `SmallGroup` / `District` / `MinistryContext` tables are historical/removed.
3. Do not switch any visibility, matching, or eligibility to `ChurchStructureMembership` outside an approved CS-CORE.2B consumer slice with targeted tests.
4. Do not treat requested memberships as access grants, in any milestone.
5. Do not make mapping review/edit the main staff setup UI; it stays migration diagnostics.
6. Do not mix structure setup with membership roster management in one UI or milestone.
7. Do not pull Community Activities (or notifications, attendance, audience-filter expansion) into CS-CORE.
8. Do not change ServiceEvent or Bible Study visibility/eligibility semantics without targeted tests; in parity milestones (1A–1C), any behavior difference is a bug.
9. Do not change reading/reflection/comment privacy in selector/refactor milestones; that area moves only in its own later CS-CORE.2B slice.
10. Do not infer serving assignments, ministry roles, staff capabilities, or permissions from membership — ever.
11. Do not let structure setup, mapping, or roster actions cascade writes into audience rows, `TeamAssignment`, `ChurchRoleAssignment`, or other modules' data.
12. Do not auto-reconcile belonging drift inside request cycles; reconciliation is an explicit, separately approved command or staff action.
